About CZINCC

Why another chamber?

To unlock the full potential of Czech–Indian economic cooperation, businesses need a focused platform that addresses real, day-to-day challenges.

Key challenges in Czech–Indian operations

Seven realities
01
Deep but underused ties
The Czech Republic and India have long-standing industrial and trade links, yet much of the potential on both sides remains untapped — especially for Czech companies investing and producing in India.
02
High entry barriers
Complex regulations, language and cultural differences, and fragmented local networks make it difficult for individual firms to enter, scale and operate efficiently.
03
Inactive and unrepresentative business networks
Existing Czech–Indian business associations are largely inactive, gather only a few passive members, do not reflect the needs of today's Czech–Indian businesses, and are barely visible to key stakeholders or at major events.
04
Outdated support model
Government support remains focused mainly on a traditional export model, which does not fully match India's Make in India strategy or the reality of Czech investment and production on the ground.
05
Limited incentives for SMEs
There are almost no targeted subsidies or incentives for Czech SMEs that want to invest, localise production or build joint ventures in India.
06
Complex foreign financing
Complicated rules and compliance requirements for foreign financing (e.g. external commercial borrowing) slow down investment decisions and expansion.
07
Restrictive visa regime
Lengthy and difficult visa procedures from India to the Czech Republic, combined with low quotas for work and business visas, limit mobility of key talent and managers.

Main functions of CZINCC

What we do
01

Peer platform for active businesses

A curated community of Czech, Indian and multinational companies already operating in both markets — sharing concrete project experience, solutions and trusted contacts.

02

Policy and advocacy voice

Coordinated representation of members' interests towards Czech and Indian authorities on issues such as visas, investment conditions, subsidies, industrial policy and market access.

03

Practical tools for doing business

Compliance and regulatory manuals, step-by-step guides, templates and checklists (tax, HR, contracts, company setup, financing, etc.) tailored specifically to Czech–Indian operations.

04

Industrial cluster & joint projects in India

Support for building a Czech industrial cluster in India — shared facilities, local supplier networks and joint promotion to make market entry and expansion faster, safer and more cost-effective.

05

HR & talent experience sharing

Exchange of best practices on recruitment, retention, intercultural management, mobility of key staff and setting up HR processes that work in both the Czech and Indian contexts.

06

Knowledge & communication network

Regular insight briefs, expert roundtables and fast everyday communication (e.g. WhatsApp groups and online forums) to keep members informed, connected and able to react quickly.
